Item of the Week: Syllabus

Description

The item I have chosen is a syllabus I received on Thursday, 1/22. The item is quite literally two pieces of paper with printed text on it and stapled together.

The paper is basic printer paper, very thin and very foldable or bendable.

The staple is metallic, very hard, but also thin—though not as thin as the paper. Considering how small the staple is, it is also bendable, but it would be a bit more difficult due to its size.

The text contains information about a course I am taking this semester with pretty much everything that I need to know. I am throwing this piece away because I believe I do not need a physical copy of the syllabus, as it is uploaded digitally.

In the moment, the syllabus was useful because I did not need to search through Canvas to find it, and everything was listed in front of me. However, I have no use for it now because I would not know where to store it, and it is more convenient to access it digitally.

Anecdote

I received this syllabus during the first week of the semester while sitting in Scott Hall. It was one of those classes where you do not know what to expect.

Most people were coming in quietly with their laptops ready, waiting for class to start. Once the professor began, it turned out to be a very laid-back class with open discussion.

As I was handed the syllabus, I flipped through it to see if I could get a head start. The professor continued to read through it while being very open to questions and confusion, explaining everything clearly so we would not be confused as the course commenced.

I am looking forward to the course. As I was packing up at the end of class, I shoved the hard copy of the syllabus into my bag.

I eventually came across it again while unpacking my bag at home and thought to myself, “Do I have any use for this anymore?” I then opted to throw it into the recycling bin.
